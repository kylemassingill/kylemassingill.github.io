Radio Instrumentation & Astrophysics

Kyle D. Massingill,
PhD

Jansky Fellow at the National Radio Astronomy Observatory. I design and test millimeter-wave and centimeter-wave instruments, with a particular focus on atmospheric monitoring for ground based astronomy. I also study high-redshift galaxies to improve our understanding of how quasar feedback regulates galaxy evolution.

Areas of Work

Research

Atmospheric Calibration

Phase Correction for Ground-Based Instruments

Development of strategies for the calibration of tropospheric water vapor using atmospheric siumulations and water vapor radiometry.

Galaxy Evolution

Quasar Feedback & Galaxy Regulation

Investigating how feedback from luminous quasars shapes and suppresses star formation, exploring the role of AGN in regulating the growth of their host galaxies.

Instrumentation

Millimeter-Wave Filter-Bank Designs

Design, simulation, and fabrication of mm-wave filter-banks integrated into novel weather sensors. Tested on high-altitude balloon platforms for atmospheric profiling.
